CHORDS

Step 1: Whether you're writing this song on guitar or piano, pick some chords that sound good in sequence and are all in the same key. Some very common keys for songs to be written in are A major, C major, and D major. The chords will be the backbone of the melody.

VOCAL MELODY

Step 2: Now pick notes that will sound pretty over the chords if you sing them. These can be notes that actually occur in the chord.
Don't worry about words yet. Those will come soon.

LYRICS

Step 3: Write some words for your song. Usually lyricists write with meter and rhyme and other nice stuff, so that the words have a structure too. Then they sound nice with the music structure.

    Common Types of Lyrics
  1. Sad
  2. Happy
  3. Romantic
  4. Aspiring
  5. Narrative

OTHER INSTRUMENTS

Step 4: Play your song with a band or whichever instrumentalists you can find. Make sure you practice a lot; don't be like my band. Then record it (maybe even in a studio) and make it sound polished.

Instruments Role
Guitarist Overall Melody
Bassist Rhythm, support the melody
Drummer Rhythm and tempo
Pianist Overall melody
Vocalist Melody of the lyrics
